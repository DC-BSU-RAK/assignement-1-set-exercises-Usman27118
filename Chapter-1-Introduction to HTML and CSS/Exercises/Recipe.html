Gourmet Chocolate Chip Cookies

These aren't your average chocolate chip cookies! With browned butter, sea salt, and premium chocolate chunks, this elevated version of the classic will become your new favorite.

Ingredients

Dry Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 1/2 tsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt

Wet Ingredients

  • 1 cup (227g) unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup (165g) packed dark brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs + 1 yolk
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

Add-ins

  • 8 oz (225g) semi-sweet chocolate chunks
  • 4 oz (115g) milk chocolate, chopped
  • Flaky sea salt for finishing

Instructions

  1. Brown the butter: Melt butter in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, swirling constantly. Continue cooking until butter turns amber in color and develops nutty aroma (about 5-7 minutes). Immediately transfer to a heatproof bowl to stop cooking.
  2. Chill butter: Let butter cool for 15 minutes at room temperature, then refrigerate for 30 minutes until it solidifies but is still soft.
  3. Prep dry ingredients: Whisk together flour, baking soda, cornstarch, and salt in a medium bowl.
  4. Cream butter and sugars: Using a stand mixer with paddle attachment, beat the browned butter with both sugars on medium speed for 2 minutes until light and fluffy.
  5. Add eggs and vanilla: Add eggs one at a time, then the yolk, mixing well after each addition. Add vanilla and mix until combined.
  6. Combine dry ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients on low speed, mixing just until incorporated.
  7. Add chocolate: Fold in chocolate chunks and chopped milk chocolate.
  8. Chill dough: Cover and chill dough for at least 2 hours (up to 72 hours for best flavor).
  9. Preheat oven: When ready to bake, preheat oven to 350¬∞F (175¬∞C) and line baking sheets with parchment.
  10. Scoop dough: Using a 2-tablespoon cookie scoop, portion dough onto sheets, spacing 3 inches apart.
  11. Bake: Bake for 10-12 minutes until edges are golden but centers look slightly underdone.
  12. Finish: Immediately sprinkle with flaky sea salt and let cool on sheets for 5 minutes before transferring to wire racks.

Baker's Tips

  • For extra flavor, brown the butter until you see dark brown specks at the bottom of the pan
  • Chilling the dough is crucial - it prevents spreading and enhances flavor
  • Use a mix of chocolate chunks and chopped chocolate for varied texture
  • Underbake slightly for soft, chewy centers
  • Dough balls can be frozen for up to 3 months - bake from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes
